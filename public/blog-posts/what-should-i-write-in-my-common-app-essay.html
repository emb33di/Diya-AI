What Should I Write in my Common App Essay?

Your golden ticket to standing out in college admissions

The Common Application essay is your golden ticket. It's the one place where you get to speak directly to the admissions committee, sharing who you are beyond your grades, test scores, and extracurriculars. It can feel daunting, but it's also an incredible opportunity. So, where do you start?

First, Choose Your Prompt (or Don't)

The Common App offers seven prompts, and they're designed to be open-ended. They cover topics from personal growth and challenging a belief to discussing something that captivates your interest.

The Secret to Prompt Selection

Here's a secret: the prompt you choose matters far less than the story you tell. Don't pick a prompt and then try to invent a story to fit it. Instead, think about the stories you already have. What moments have defined you? What experiences have shaped your perspective? Once you have a story in mind, you'll likely find it fits perfectly into one (or more) of the prompts. Think of the prompts as doorways; you just need to choose the one that your story opens most naturally.

What Actually Needs to Be in the Essay?

Once you have a story in mind, how do you shape it into a compelling 650-word essay? Here are five key things to focus on.

  • Tell a Story About You

    The whole point of the essay is to paint a picture of who you are, what you hope to become, and how you got to be the person you are today. This isn't the place to re-list your resume. It's the place to connect the dots. An essay that describes your passion for physics is good, but an essay that tells the story of how dismantling your grandfather's old radio sparked that passion is great. It reveals curiosity, dedication, and a personal connection.

  • Make it Interesting!

    You are competing for the attention of an admissions officer who reads hundreds, if not thousands, of essays. Make yours stand out! If you have a unique hobby or a fascinating experience, don't be afraid to share it. For instance, if you played a sport professionally or spent a summer learning the art of beekeeping, those are fantastic, memorable details that make your application unique. An interesting story is a memorable one.

  • Be Personal and Vulnerable

    Some of the most powerful essays come from personal experience. If you have a touching story from your past that reveals how you've evolved, share it. It shows maturity and self-awareness. For example, perhaps you overcame a childhood stutter. Don't just state that you had a stutter; tell the story of how overcoming it made you a more confident public speaker, leading you to join the debate team or volunteer to give school tours. Turn a past challenge into a present strength.

  • It's All About How You Frame It

    While personal stories are powerful, it's important to strike the right balance. Admissions committees don't want to hear exclusively about the hardships you've faced. If you do choose to write about a difficult experience, the focus should not be on the hardship itself, but on your response to it. How did it change you? What did you learn? How did you grow? The story should always be a vehicle for telling the reader something insightful about you.

  • Structure, Structure, Structure

    A great story can fall flat without a solid structure. Three things are essential:

    An Opening Hook

    Your first sentence needs to grab the reader's attention and make them want to keep reading.

    Good Transitions

    Your essay should flow logically from one paragraph to the next. Each paragraph should build on the one before it, creating a seamless narrative arc.

    A Narrative Style

    Think like a storyteller. Use vivid language, show instead of just telling, and create a narrative that has a beginning, a middle, and a satisfying conclusion.

    • Final Thoughts

    Crafting the perfect Common App essay is a journey of self-reflection. Take your time, be authentic, and tell the story that only you can tell.
